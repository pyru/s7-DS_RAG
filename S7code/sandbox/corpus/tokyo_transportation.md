# Tokyo Transportation System Guide

Tokyo's public transportation network is the world's most extensive and punctual. Understanding how to navigate it efficiently is key to a productive trip.

## The IC Card: Your Essential Tool

The **Suica** (JR East) and **Pasmo** (Tokyo Metro) IC cards are rechargeable contactless cards accepted on all trains, subways, buses, monorails, and at many convenience stores, vending machines, and cafes.

Load yen at ticket machines or convenience stores. Tap the card reader at the gate — entry and exit is logged automatically; fare is calculated at exit. If you underpay (insufficient balance), the gates won't open; top up at a fare adjustment machine.

**Mobile Suica**: Add to Apple Pay or Google Pay for even more convenience. Works worldwide with your phone.

**Foreign visitor IC cards**: As of 2024, IC cards can be reloaded with foreign credit/debit cards at many machines. The Welcome Suica (for foreign visitors) has a 28-day expiry and no refund on balance — useful for short trips.

## JR Lines

The Japan Railways (JR) network includes:

**Yamanote Line (環状線)**: The circular loop connecting all major city hubs — Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Ebisu, Osaki, Shinagawa, Tokyo, Akihabara, Ueno, Ikebukuro. Trains run every 3-5 minutes, 24h except late night. Memorize this line.

**Chuo/Sobu Lines**: Cross the city east-west. Express (Chuo Rapid) and local (Chuo-Sobu Local) versions.

**JR Pass**: Weekly/multi-week pass covering all JR Shinkansen and local lines nationwide. Cost-effective for multi-city trips but requires advance purchase outside Japan. Only the Narita Express and some Shinkansen routes require it for significant savings within Tokyo.

## Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway

13 subway lines operated by two companies (Tokyo Metro and Toei) crisscross the city. Both accept IC cards; a single-ride ticket works on both systems.

Key lines for tourists:
- **Ginza Line (G)**: Oldest line, connecting Asakusa to Shibuya
- **Hibiya Line (H)**: Roppongi, Akihabara, Ueno
- **Marunouchi Line (M)**: Tokyo Station, Shinjuku, Ikebukuro
- **Fukutoshin Line (F)**: Shinjuku, Ikebukuro → connects to Tokyu Toyoko Line to Yokohama

## Bus Network

Tokyo's bus network is extensive but harder to navigate (stops announced in Japanese only). Most useful route:
- **Toei Bus 01**: Shibuya → Nihonbashi passing through central districts
- **Meguro-Sanchome circular**: Useful for Meguro River sakura area

## Taxis and Ride-Sharing

Taxis are expensive but useful late at night or for heavy luggage. Hail on street or use the GO app (most widely accepted). Doors open automatically — do not touch.

Uber operates in Tokyo but is more expensive than local apps and has limited coverage.

## Airport Access

**Narita Airport (NRT)**:
- Narita Express (N'EX): 1h to Tokyo, Shinjuku, Shibuya (¥3,070)
- Keisei Skyliner: 36min to Ueno (¥2,520)

**Haneda Airport (HND)**: Closer, better connected
- Tokyo Monorail: 13min to Hamamatsucho
- Keikyu Line: 20min to Shinagawa, Sengakuji
