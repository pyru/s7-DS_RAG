# Tokyo Activities and Things to Do

Tokyo, Japan's capital, offers an extraordinary range of activities spanning ancient traditions and futuristic innovation. With over 13 million residents in the city proper and 37 million in the greater metropolitan area, it is the world's most populous urban agglomeration.

## Cultural and Historical Sites

**Senso-ji Temple** in Asakusa is Tokyo's oldest and most visited temple, founded in 645 CE. The iconic Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) leads to Nakamise shopping street, lined with vendors selling traditional crafts and snacks. Visit early morning to avoid crowds and see the gate dramatically lit at dawn.

**Meiji Jingu Shrine** in Harajuku honors Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, set within 70 hectares of forested parkland. The wooded approach offers tranquil relief from the city's energy. Arrive on Sunday mornings to witness traditional wedding processions.

**Edo-Tokyo Museum** in Ryogoku depicts the city's history from the Edo period to modernity through detailed reconstructions, life-size replicas of old buildings, and thousands of artifacts.

## Modern Tokyo Experiences

**Shibuya Crossing** — the world's busiest pedestrian intersection — is best observed from the second floor of Starbucks or the rooftop of Shibuya Sky observatory. Up to 3,000 people cross simultaneously when the light changes.

**Akihabara** (Electric Town) is the center of Tokyo's electronics and anime culture. Multi-floor shops sell components, gadgets, vintage games, manga, and merchandise from every era of Japanese pop culture.

**teamLab Borderless** and **teamLab Planets** are immersive digital art museums where visitors walk through projection-mapped environments that respond to movement. Book tickets weeks in advance.

## Food Experiences

Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world. Must-try experiences include:
- **Tsukiji Outer Market**: fresh tuna sashimi and tamagoyaki (egg omelets) for breakfast
- **Ramen museums** in Shinjuku and Yokohama
- **Izakayas** (Japanese pub-restaurants) in Shinjuku's Golden Gai or Yurakucho
- **Depachika** (department store basement food halls) at Isetan or Takashimaya

## Day Trips from Tokyo

- **Nikko**: ornate shrines and waterfall gorges (2 hours by train)
- **Kamakura**: Great Buddha statue and coastal shrines (1 hour by train)
- **Hakone**: Mt. Fuji views, hot spring ryokan, open-air sculpture museum
- **Kyoto**: ancient capital with 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sites (2.5 hours by shinkansen)

## Practical Tips

The **IC card** (Suica or Pasmo) is essential — load it with yen and use it on all trains, subways, and many convenience stores. Google Maps provides excellent real-time transit directions in English.
