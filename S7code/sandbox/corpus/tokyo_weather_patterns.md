# Tokyo Weather Patterns and Seasonal Guide

Tokyo experiences four distinct seasons with significant variation in temperature, humidity, and precipitation. Understanding seasonal patterns is essential for planning an enjoyable visit.

## Spring (March – May): Cherry Blossom Season

Spring is widely considered the best time to visit Tokyo. Temperatures range from 10°C (50°F) in early March to 22°C (72°F) in May, with mild humidity.

**Cherry blossom (sakura) season** typically runs from late March to early April. Peak bloom (mankai) lasts only 1–2 weeks and depends on yearly temperature patterns. In 2024, peak bloom was March 25; in 2023 it was March 22. The Japan Meteorological Corporation issues annual bloom forecasts from January.

Top hanami (cherry blossom viewing) spots:
- Ueno Park (1,200 trees)
- Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden
- Chidorigafuchi moat (illuminated evenings)
- Meguro River

Crowds are significant during sakura season, so book accommodation months in advance.

## Summer (June – August): Hot and Humid

Summer brings Tokyo's most challenging weather. June marks the **tsuyu** (rainy season), with overcast skies and daily showers lasting through mid-July. Temperatures then climb to 30–35°C (86–95°F) with extreme humidity (70–80%), making the heat feel oppressive.

Practical advice for summer:
- Stay hydrated — convenience stores sell sports drinks everywhere
- Visit indoor attractions: museums, teamLab, department stores
- Carry a portable fan and cooling towel
- Summer festivals (**matsuri**) with fireworks displays occur throughout July and August
- Obon week in mid-August sees reduced crowds at tourist sites as locals travel to hometowns

## Autumn (September – November): Ideal Weather

Autumn offers comfortable temperatures of 15–25°C (59–77°F), low humidity, and vivid foliage colors. This rivals spring as the best season to visit.

**Koyo** (autumn leaf viewing) peaks in late November:
- Shinjuku Gyoen: ginkgo trees turn brilliant gold
- Rikugien Garden: illuminated evening foliage events
- Nikko: red maples against ancient cedar forests (day trip from Tokyo)

September can bring **typhoons** (tropical storms) that occasionally disrupt transportation. Check the Japan Meteorological Agency (JMA) website for real-time storm tracking.

## Winter (December – February): Cold but Clear

Winter temperatures range from 2–12°C (36–54°F). Snow falls 1–5 times per season, typically light accumulation that melts quickly. The air is dry and clear — the best visibility for Mt. Fuji views from Hakone or the Bunkyo Civic Center observation deck.

Winter advantages:
- Smallest tourist crowds of any season
- Christmas illuminations across Roppongi, Marunouchi, and Shibuya
- New Year (Oshogatsu) traditions at temples (hatsumode — first shrine visit)
- Less competitive accommodation pricing

## Packing Recommendations by Season

| Season | Essentials |
|--------|-----------|
| Spring | Light layers, umbrella, comfortable walking shoes |
| Summer | Light clothing, powerful sunscreen, cooling towel |
| Autumn | Light jacket, layers for temperature swings |
| Winter | Warm coat, scarf, gloves |

Japan's train and indoor spaces are reliably climate-controlled year-round, so dress in layers you can remove.
